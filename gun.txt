1. THE PROBLEM IT SOLVES
----------------------------------------------------------------------
* Traditional Bolt-Actions: High precision, but slow fire rate.
* Gas-Operated Semi-Autos: Fast fire rate, but gas tubes/pistons 
  introduce barrel vibration and degrade long-range accuracy.
* The Solution: This design achieves zero downtime and high fire 
  rates using a mechanical hand-crank and chain drive, keeping the 
  single barrel 100% rigid and free of gas distortion.

2. SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE
----------------------------------------------------------------------
* 1 x Single, Heavy-Mass Fluted Precision Barrel (Max Thermal Mass)
* 2 x Independent Bolt-Action Cores (Core A & Core B)
* 1 x Reciprocating Lateral Shuttle Tray (Typewriter Slide Layout)
* 1 x Continuous Chain Drive Loop connected to a Manual Hand Crank
* 1 x Star-Wheel Ammunition Belt Feed Hub

3. THE 360-DEGREE MECHANICAL CYCLE (Per Crank Turn)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
[0° to 90°]   -> SHUTTLE SWAP: Cam slides the tray horizontally. 
                 Loaded Core A aligns perfectly with the single barrel. 
                 Fired Core B shifts outward to the side cooling port.

[90° to 180°] -> STRIKE: Rotary striker lobe hits the trigger sear. 
                 Core A firing pin releases. Round fires down the 
                 rigid, precision barrel.

[180° to 270°]-> RESET & EXTRACT: Linear push-rod connected to the 
                 crank yanks Core B backward. Spent shell is extracted 
                 and ejected. Core B spring is cocked/reset.

[270° to 360°]-> INDEX: A star-wheel gear pulls the ammunition chain 
                 forward by 1 bullet, feeding it directly into the 
                 open path of the newly reset Core B.
